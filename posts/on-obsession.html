On Obsession

All the accomplishments in my life have come from an obsession.

When I was 9 years old, I stumbled upon the idea that a single person could commit the entire Quran to memory. This absolutely blew my mind. A book I could barely read could be memorized? And it was a fat one too. We had those 13-liners that are about 5x7, so it was pretty condensed and thicc.

Anyway, looking back, this was the root of my first obsession, but it hadn’t yet taken hold of me.

That summer, in 2005, my family visited back home, Dhaka, and they arranged a Quran teacher for me. Yeah, whatever. No kid wants that on his vacation, but again, whatever. (You’ll see that phrase a lot in my life.) Imagine my brain when I found out my very own Quran teacher was one of those people who had memorized the whole Quran. That was it. I decided I had to become one too.

That was my first obsession. Fast-forward a few years and I was in a full-time madrasa memorizing Quran with like 20 other boys. Some were 15 or 16 while I was 12. If you understand boys, then you immediately see the problem here. But anyway, I’d obsessed my way into becoming a Hafiz.

Looking back at the few years of my life after I became a Hafiz and before I started university, I can say I was missing a true mental obsession. I’d joined the football team and hated it, yet I played all four years as a lineman because I was one of the bigger boys. Why did I do that? Whenever I thought of quitting, I guess my internal monologue was like, eh, whatever, I’ll just see it through. Things that aren’t obsessed over often end this way for me.

Fast-forward to university, and I was struck with this fucking anxiety disorder. It completely consumed like a decade of my life. But I have enough thoughts on that for a whole book. Maybe one day I’ll write one? I would hate for anyone to go through that decade. To be kitsch, I would absolutely call it My Lost Decade.

The funny thing about obsessing is that it completely consumes you, and I love that. It helped me lose 90 pounds, gain 150, lose it all, and now be a fat fuck weighing almost 300 pounds. Friends and family have asked, “What the hell is going on?” I don’t have a good answer. See title for more.

I have a very strong feeling my life would feel empty if I didn’t constantly obsess over something. I’m not sure what it is at the moment, which is actually a little insightful for my current state. Hmmm.

Maybe this personal project of mine will become one?
