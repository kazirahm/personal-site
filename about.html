About

I build systems and chase big questions.

I’m Kazi Rahman, a senior software engineer at Meta working on security infrastructure for AI systems. My broader background is in distributed backend systems, caching, data infrastructure, and the unglamorous engineering that keeps important software reliable.

San Francisco Bay Area · B.S. Computer Science, UC Santa Cruz

The work

I like problems where the answer has to survive reality: enormous traffic, partial failures, ambiguous incidents, tight latency budgets, and many teams depending on the same infrastructure.

Over the years, I’ve worked across large-scale caching and storage systems, real-time analytics, advertising platforms, healthcare software, and security tooling. The common thread is building dependable foundations—then making them faster, safer, and easier for other engineers to use.

I’m especially interested in the intersection of AI agents, authorization, policy, and infrastructure security: how we give increasingly capable systems useful access without losing control of what they can do.

The questions

Software is rarely just software. It encodes assumptions about power, incentives, trust, and human behavior. That is why my interests keep spilling into history, philosophy, political economy, psychology, and religion.

I’m drawn to questions that resist neat answers: how institutions gain legitimacy, how technology changes the way people live, what makes a life meaningful, and how Islamic thought can speak seriously to modernity without simply imitating or rejecting it.

This site is where those interests become obsession driven development: following a question until it turns into an essay, a project, a useful idea, or at least a better question.

Away from the keyboard

I live in the Bay Area with my wife and two cats. I’m usually rotating between a history or philosophy rabbit hole, football, a video game with unusually satisfying mechanics, fitness, and a home project that looked simpler before I started it.

I’m a Muslim, a first-generation American, and an incurably curious generalist. I care about ambition, but also about understanding what ambition is for.
